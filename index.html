Why Morocco?

Present-day Morocco has been inhabited since at least Paleolithic times. Some research suggests inhabitants have been there since early Homo Sapiens roughly 315,000 years ago. This extensive history makes Morocco a country with a rich culture and civilisation. Through its' history, it has hosted many people coming from East (Phoenicians, Jews and Arabs), South (Sub-Saharan Africans) and North (Romans, Andalusians). All those civilisations have affected the social structure of Morocco.

Morocco is widely varied in architecture, art, and cuisine. Morocco's architecture is incredibly diverse, influenced by the ancient Romans it once hosted as well as more modern influences of French and Spanish design.
Moroccan cuisine is considered one of the most diversified cuisines in the world. In this fusion of Moorish, European and Mediterranean cuisines, there are many incredible, flavorful dishes to explore. Try a Tajine filled with aromatic meat, veggies, and spices, or a plate of couscous, a staple of the Berber peoples of the region. There's no wrong choice!
